## 2. Company Overview & Legal Structure

### 2.1 Entity Selection
The company will be registered as a **Private Limited Company (Pvt. Ltd.)** under the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA), India.
*   **Why?** Essential for raising venture capital, issuing equity (ESOPs), and limiting liability—crucial when dealing with high-risk events and international contracts.

### 2.2 Registration & Government Fees
*   **Incorporation Process (SPICe+ Portal):** Name reservation, Digital Signature Certificates (DSC), Director Identification Number (DIN), MoA, and AoA.
*   **Government Fees:** ₹15,000 - ₹25,000 (depending on authorized capital & state stamp duty).
*   **Professional Fees (CA/CS):** ₹20,000 - ₹30,000.
*   **DPIIT Startup India Registration:** Post-incorporation, we will register with the Department for Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT) to access tax holidays, angel tax exemptions (Section 56), and fast-tracked IP registration.
*   **Trademark Registration:** ₹9,000 per class (Class 35 for business management, Class 41 for entertainment).

## 4. Statutory Compliance & Operating Regulations

Booking international artists in India is heavily regulated. Our business plan accounts for strict compliance, turning regulatory hurdles into a moat.

### 4.1 Cross-Border Taxation (TDS & Income Tax)
*   **Section 195 (TDS on Non-Residents):** Any payment to a foreign artist is subject to withholding tax at the source. Standard rates can be 20%+.
*   **Double Taxation Avoidance Agreement (DTAA):** We will require all international artists to provide a **Tax Residency Certificate (TRC)** and **Form 10F** to avail of lower tax rates (usually 10-15%).
*   **PAN Requirement:** Assisting artists in obtaining an Indian Permanent Account Number (PAN) via our legal partners to prevent punitive withholding rates.

### 4.2 FEMA & RBI Guidelines
*   **Foreign Remittances:** Governed by the Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA).
*   **Procedure:** Remittances require routing through an Authorized Dealer (AD) Category-I Bank, supported by the Artist Contract, and **Form 15CA & 15CB** (Chartered Accountant certification of tax deduction).

### 4.3 Goods & Services Tax (GST)
*   The company will obtain a regular GST registration.
*   **Output GST:** 18% on platform fees, booking agency commissions, and event tickets.
*   **Input Tax Credit (ITC):** Claimable on venue rentals, tech server costs, and vendor services.

### 4.4 Event Licensing & Permits (Ground Operations)
Because we operate at the intersection of a tech platform and event execution, ensuring local compliance is mandatory:
*   **Music Licenses:** PPL (sound recordings), IPRS (live performance/lyrics), Novex.
*   **NOCs:** Local Police NOC, Traffic NOC, and Fire Department Safety NOC.
*   **Excise / Liquor Licensing:** 1-day temporary club/event licenses (FL-4) if we colocate or co-promote.
*   **Foreigners Regional Registration Office (FRRO):** Ensuring proper Business/Employment Visas (not tourist visas) are secured for performing artists.

## 7. Business Model & Revenue Streams

1.  **Platform Marketplace Fee (SaaS/Commission):**
    *   Taking a flat percentage (e.g., 5-10%) on every booking successfully negotiated and contracted through the platform. 
2.  **Agency / Promoter Margin (Proprietary Event Income):**
    *   When acting as the primary buyer/promoter for international artists, we keep the net profit from ticket sales, F&B cuts (negotiated with venues), and sponsorships.
3.  **Premium SaaS Subscriptions for Venues:**
    *   Monthly fee for venues to access advanced analytics, premium placements, and contract templates.
4.  **Sponsorships & Brand Partnerships:**
    *   Liquor, apparel, and lifestyle brands targeting the underground demographic.
