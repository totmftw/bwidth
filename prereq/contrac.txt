1) What you are really building

Think of the system as five connected parts:

The artist is profiled and categorized.
The organizer posts a booking opportunity.
The artist applies or the organizer shortlists.
Both sides agree on terms and the platform generates a contract.
Payments, cancellation rules, and trust updates follow that contract.

Your existing docs already support most of this chain: opportunities, applications, negotiation, booking, contracts, payments, trust score, and cancellation logic are all part of the intended platform design.

2) What is missing right now

You already have some implicit artist grouping in the app. In programming mode, artists are being categorized as headliner, support, and emerging based on fee vs weekly budget, and match scoring already uses genre, trust, location, and cancellation history. But that is not the same as the business-facing categories you described: budding, mid-scale, and international.

So the missing piece is:

a real stored category on the artist profile,
a rule engine that assigns or overrides that category,
a commission policy that reads that category,
and a contract template that changes based on category and trust.
3) The simplest business rule

Use two separate ideas:

Artist category = who the artist is in the market
Example: budding / mid-scale / international.
Trust score tier = how reliable the artist is on the platform
Example: critical / high risk / standard / trusted / premium.

Your docs already use trust score to drive platform commission and application limits, so keep that as a separate layer. The trust score tiers in the workflow already map to different commission rates and limits, while your artist category should control commercial positioning and contract language.

5) How artist categorization should work

Do this in two phases.

Phase 1: automatic suggestion

When an artist completes onboarding, the system suggests a category using:

fee range,
experience level,
trust score,
portfolio completeness,
and optionally manual review.

A simple rule can be:

budding: early-stage, low-fee, limited bookings
mid-scale: established local/regional talent
international: premium/high-value talent

Your docs already collect enough data for this: fee bands, experience, portfolio, location, documents, and trust score.

Phase 2: admin confirmation or override

For the first version, let an admin confirm the category. That prevents bad automatic tagging when the profile is incomplete.

Recommended rule:

auto-suggest on onboarding
admin can approve or override
category gets locked for premium/international artists
category can be reviewed every 90 days
6) How commission should work

Your meeting notes describe a double-sided monetization model: the platform charges both artist and organizer, and the platform keeps the spread. That is separate from the trust-score-based commission logic currently in the docs, which is a single platform commission percentage tied to reliability.

For your app, use this structure:

Recommended settlement model

For every booking, store:

organizer payable
artist receivable
platform fee from organizer
platform fee from artist
net payout to artist
net cost to organizer

That makes the pricing transparent internally, even if you choose to show only the final price on the UI.

Example

If the booking value is ₹100:

organizer pays ₹125
artist receives ₹75
platform keeps ₹50

That matches the model from your agenda.

Category-based logic

Use category to decide the base split:

budding: higher platform share, but protected minimum earnings
mid-scale: balanced split
international: lower percentage on the artist side, higher absolute revenue through premium fee
Trust-score modifier

Then apply a small modifier from trust tier:

critical/high risk: stricter payment terms
standard/trusted/premium: more flexible deposit rules

Your docs already use trust score to change commission rate and advance payment behavior.

7) The contract flow you should implement

Use this exact flow:

Step 1: Booking is confirmed

The organizer accepts, or negotiation ends successfully. Your docs already route acceptance to booking creation and then contract generation.

Step 2: Contract is generated

The contract service loads:

artist profile
organizer profile
venue
booking
trust tier
artist category
payment split
cancellation rules

Your current contract generator already prepares party details, event details, financial terms, hospitality, promotion, confidentiality, and force majeure clauses.

Step 3: Contract is sent to both sides

Your workflow already says:

email
in-app notification
sign button in dashboard
signing window
signature verification

That is the right pattern.

Step 4: Artist signs first

The docs say the artist signs first, then the organizer signs. That sequence is good because it locks the artist’s consent before the organizer finalizes the show.

Step 5: Organizer signs

Once the organizer signs, contract status becomes fully executed.

Step 6: Payment schedule is created

Your payment service already sets up milestone payments after contract signing, including a 30% deposit and balance due before the event. You can keep that pattern, but make the percentages configurable by artist category and trust tier.

12) Very important implementation choice

Do not calculate commission live from the current artist profile after the contract is signed. Always save the exact numbers into the booking and contract at the moment of confirmation.

That matters because your docs already include trust score changes, cancellations, and category-based scheduling logic. If you recalculate later, old contracts will drift when the artist’s profile changes.
